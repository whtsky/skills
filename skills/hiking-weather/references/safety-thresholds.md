# Hiking Safety Thresholds

## Go / No-Go Decision Factors

### Wind

| Summit wind speed | Assessment |
|-------------------|------------|
| < 30 km/h | ✅ Comfortable |
| 30–50 km/h | ⚠️ Windy, exposed ridges challenging |
| 50–70 km/h | 🔴 Dangerous on exposed terrain, consider postponing |
| > 70 km/h | ❌ Do not proceed on exposed routes |

Wind gusts are typically 1.5–2x sustained speed. If gusts > 80 km/h on exposed ridges, turn back.

### Visibility

| Visibility | Assessment |
|------------|------------|
| > 10 km | ✅ Clear, full panoramic views |
| 5–10 km | ✅ Good, some haze |
| 1–5 km | ⚠️ Reduced, navigation harder on unmarked routes |
| 200m–1 km | 🔴 Poor, stay on well-marked trails only |
| < 200m | ❌ Whiteout conditions, do not proceed |

### Precipitation

| Condition | Assessment |
|-----------|------------|
| 0% probability | ✅ Dry |
| < 30% light rain | ✅ Manageable with rain gear |
| 30–60% moderate rain | ⚠️ Wet conditions, slippery rocks |
| > 60% heavy rain/snow | 🔴 Consider postponing |
| Thunderstorm forecast | ❌ Do not go above treeline |

### Freezing Level

Compare freezing level height to route max elevation:
- Freezing level well above summit → no ice risk
- Freezing level near summit → ice possible on shaded slopes, bring microspikes
- Freezing level below summit → ice/snow likely, crampons may be needed

### Cloud Cover by Altitude

This is key for view assessment:
- Cloud cover at pressure levels BELOW summit → clouds beneath you (cloud sea! 🌊)
- Cloud cover at summit level → in the clouds = fog, no view
- Cloud cover ABOVE summit → overcast but views possible

### Wind Chill (Apparent Temperature)

| Apparent temp | Gear needed |
|---------------|-------------|
| > 15°C | Light layers |
| 5–15°C | Mid layers, wind shell |
| 0–5°C | Warm layers, wind/waterproof shell |
| -5–0°C | Full winter layers, gloves, hat |
| < -5°C | Serious cold weather gear |

## Time-of-Day Considerations

- **Early morning (sunrise–9am)**: Typically calmest winds, clearest skies, coldest temps
- **Midday (10am–2pm)**: Thermal convection builds → cumulus clouds form, wind picks up
- **Afternoon (2pm–sunset)**: Highest chance of afternoon thunderstorms in summer, worst visibility from orographic clouds
- **Best strategy**: Start early, be at/past the summit by midday, descend in the afternoon
